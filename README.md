# Video-game-sales-analysis
# Video Game Global Sales Analysis (1980–2016)

## Objective
Analysed 16,598 video game titles to identify sales trends 
across genres, platforms, regions and publishers.

## Dataset
- Source: Kaggle — vgsales.csv by Gregory Smith (VGChartz)
- Records: 16,598 titles | Period: 1980–2016

## Tools Used
Microsoft Excel — SUMIF, COUNTIF, RANK, IF, Charts

## Key Findings
- Action is the #1 genre globally with $1,745M+ in sales
- Global sales peaked in 2008–09, declined post-2010 due 
  to shift toward digital ownership
- North America = 49% of global sales; Japan uniquely 
  prefers RPGs over Action/Sports
- Nintendo generates 2x more revenue per title than EA 
  despite releasing half the titles
- Mobile gaming (invisible in this dataset) was already 
  a $36B industry by 2016 — showing the data captures 
  a shrinking slice of a growing market

## Sheets
1. Raw Data — 500-record clean sample
2. Genre Analysis — Sales by genre + regional split
3. Platform Rankings — Top 12 platforms
4. Sales Trend — YoY growth 1995–2016
5. Best Selling Games — Best-sellers all time
6. Regional Analysis — NA vs EU vs Japan
7. KPI Dashboard — Executive summary
8. Observations — Qualitative insights beyond the data
